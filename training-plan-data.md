## Monday — Upper Push + Power (~55 min)

### 1. Kettlebell Halo

- **Sets × Reps:** 2 × 12 each direction
- **Weight:** 20 kg
- **Tempo:** Slow, controlled
- **Rest:** None — go straight into bench
- **New:** No
- **Why:** Warms up shoulders and thoracic spine.
- **Cues:** Keep elbows tight. Circle close to head. Engage core.
- **Progression:** Move to 24 kg when 12 reps feel easy.
- **Video:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=13SFATc-mJ4

### 2. Bench Press (Tempo)

- **Sets × Reps:** 4 × 6 @ RPE 7–8
- **Weight W1:** 77.5 kg (≈72% 1RM)
- **Weight W2:** 80 kg
- **Weight W3:** 82.5 kg
- **Tempo:** 3-1-X-0 (3s down, 1s pause on chest, explode up, no pause at top)
- **Rest:** 2:30 min
- **New:** No
- **Why:** Primary horizontal push. Tempo-controlled for tendon health.
- **Cues:** 3s eccentric, 1s pause on chest, explode up. Retract shoulder blades. Feet flat.
- **Progression:** Add 2.5 kg when all 4 sets hit 6 reps at RPE 7.
- **Note:** Lighter than usual 80×12 but the 3s eccentric + pause changes the stimulus entirely.
- **Video:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rT7DgCr-3pg

### 3. Plyometric Push-Ups

- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 5
- **Load:** Bodyweight (91 kg)
- **Rest:** 90 sec
- **Key metric:** Height of hand lift, not reps
- **New:** Yes
- **Why:** Power output declines ~2× faster than strength after 40. Low-rep explosive work counters this.
- **Cues:** Hands leave ground at top. Land soft with slight elbow bend. Full reset between reps. No bouncing.
- **Progression:** Standard → clap → double clap. Stay at 3×5, never grind reps.
- **Visual positions:** Down → Explode → Airborne (hands off ground)
- **Video:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=FFlAcBTd_K0

### 4. Ring Dips

- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 8
- **Load:** Bodyweight (91 kg)
- **Rest:** 90 sec
- **Fallback:** Parallel bars if rings unavailable
- **New:** Yes
- **Why:** Rings let wrists rotate naturally — more shoulder-friendly. Adds stabilizer demand.
- **Cues:** Turn rings out slightly at top (RTO). 2s descent minimum. Full lockout.
- **Progression:** BW 3×8 → BW 3×10 → +5 kg vest → work back to 3×10.
- **Note:** With bench at 80×12, 8 ring dips should be manageable. If not, instability is the limiter — that's what we want to train.
- **Visual positions:** Top (RTO) → Bottom
- **Video:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=AdUfoCj3COQ

### 5. Floor Press

- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 10
- **Weight:** 2 × 28 kg dumbbells (start conservative)
- **Tempo:** 2s down, touch floor, press
- **Rest:** 90 sec
- **New:** Yes
- **Why:** Limits ROM to protect shoulders while overloading triceps. Great for 50+ lifters.
- **Cues:** Elbows touch ground gently each rep. No bouncing. Use dumbbells for independent arm work.
- **Progression:** +2.5 kg per DB when all sets hit 10.
- **Note:** Floor press ROM is shorter — expect to handle ~70% of flat bench equivalent with DBs.
- **Visual positions:** Elbows down → Press up
- **Video:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=ptpmRrzRtWQ

### 6. Heavy Rope Finisher

- **Sets × Reps:** 5 × 40s on / 20s off (~5 min total)
- **Rope:** 1 lb heavy rope
- **HR target:** >160 bpm during work
- **New:** No
- **Why:** Standard 30/30 too easy for 1h+ rope endurance. Shortened rest to actually challenge.
- **Cues:** Max consistent rhythm each interval. Double-unders if available.
- **Progression:** 5×40s/20s → 5×50s/10s → 6×40s/20s.

**Day note:** Tempo matters: 3s eccentric, 1s pause, explode. The bench weight feels lighter than usual — intentional. RPE 7–8 with tempo is harder than it looks.

## Tuesday — Rowing Engine + Core & Mobility (~50 min)

### 1. 30 Min Rowing Fartlek

- **Sets × Reps:** 30 min continuous (1 min max sprint every 5 minutes)
- **Target pace:** Max power during sprints, active recovery in between
- **Damper:** 5–6 (at 1.93m height)
- **Stroke rate:** 26–28 spm
- **Total distance:** ~7,000m work (logged cumulatively)
- **New:** No
- **Why:** Fartlek-style interval training. Builds VO2 max during the sprints, while maintaining a strong aerobic base during recovery.
- **Cues:** Hit true max power for 1 minute. Slow down but stay in motion for the 4-minute active recovery.
- **Progression:** Increase your total distance logged over the full 30 minutes.
- **Note:** At 91 kg and 1.93m, reaching 7km over 30 minutes is an incredibly strong benchmark.
- **Video:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zQ82RYIFLN8

### 2. Kettlebell Halo

- **Sets × Reps:** 2 × 10 each direction
- **Weight:** 20 kg
- **New:** No
- **Why:** Active recovery. Transition to core work.
- **Cues:** Slow and controlled.
- **Progression:** Use 24 kg when easy.

### 3. Dead Hang

- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 45 sec (~4 min with rest)
- **Load:** Bodyweight (91 kg)
- **Rest:** 60 sec
- **New:** Yes
- **Why:** Decompresses spine after rowing. Builds grip. Shoulder health.
- **Cues:** Fully relax — let gravity stretch you. Shoulders by ears is fine (passive). Breathe deeply. No squirming.
- **Progression:** 45s → 60s → 75s → single-arm assisted (20s each).
- **Note:** Pull-up strength says grip isn't a limiter. Focus on relaxation, not survival.
- **Visual positions:** Passive hang (relax everything)
- **Video:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=M25ibDAVhMQ

### 4. L-Sit Progression

- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 15–20 sec hold (~3 min with rest)
- **Equipment:** Parallettes or dip bars
- **Start with:** Tuck L-sit (knees bent) if full is too hard
- **Rest:** 60 sec
- **New:** Yes
- **Why:** Deep core engagement that sit-ups never reach. Builds hip flexor strength.
- **Cues:** Push shoulders down hard. Tuck knees first if needed.
- **Progression:** Tuck 20s → one leg extended 15s → full L-sit.
- **Note:** Being 1.93m makes L-sits significantly harder (longer lever arms). Don't compare to shorter people. Tuck is fine.
- **Visual positions:** Tuck → Full L-Sit
- **Video:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IUZJoSP66HI

### 5. Pallof Press

- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 10 each side
- **Equipment:** Cable machine (preferred here — constant tension)
- **Cable height:** Chest height (~130 cm)
- **Weight:** 15 kg (start)
- **Hold:** 2 sec at full extension
- **Rest:** 45 sec between sides
- **New:** Yes
- **Why:** Anti-rotation core exercise. Trains core's actual job: resisting unwanted movement. Crucial for spinal health.
- **Cues:** Press out, hold 2s, return. Resist rotation — don't let cable pull you.
- **Progression:** Increase weight 2.5 kg when 10 reps × 2s hold feels easy.
- **Note:** This is where cable machines earn their place. Free weights can't replicate this tension curve.
- **Visual positions:** Hold → Press out (resist pull)
- **Video:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AH_QZLm_0-s

### 6. 90/90 Hip Stretch

- **Duration:** 2 min each side
- **New:** Yes
- **Why:** Hip mobility degrades silently after 40. Best position for internal + external rotation.
- **Cues:** Front shin parallel to shoulders, 90° knee. Back leg 90° behind. Sit tall, lean forward gently.
- **Progression:** Add gentle rotation and forward fold over front shin.
- **Note:** At 1.93m, tight hips affect everything downstream.
- **Visual positions:** Setup (front 90° / back 90°)
- **Video:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=BNWLBuohUGQ

**Day note:** VO2max supports aggressive pacing. 1:58–2:05 is working range at 91 kg / 1.93m. Mobility work is non-negotiable.

## Wednesday — HIIT Complexes (~40 min)

Format: 3 complexes, 3 rounds each, 2 min rest between complexes.

### Complex A: Rope & Burpee

- **Rounds:** 3
- **Work:** 45s heavy rope skip → 6 burpees → 15s rest (~4 min per complex)
- **Rope:** 1 lb heavy rope
- **New:** No
- **Why:** Full-body conditioning. Intervals pushed harder than standard because 1h+ rope endurance.
- **Cues:** Stay smooth on rope after burpee fatigue.
- **Progression:** +5s rope per round. Then +2 burpees.

### Complex B: Kettlebell Circuit

- **Rounds:** 3
- **Work:** 5 clean & press each arm → 12 swings (Russian) → 5 goblet squats (2s pause) → 30s rest (~5–6 min per complex)
- **KB weight:** 20 kg (transition to 24 kg when clean & press feels solid)
- **New:** Yes
- **Why:** Clean & press is full-body power, easier on wrists than barbell cleans.
- **Cues:** Clean: rack bell smoothly, don't bang forearm. Press: full lockout. Swings: snap hips.
- **Progression:** 20 kg → 24 kg for all movements. Then +2 reps each.
- **Visual positions:** Clean (rack position) → Press (overhead lockout)
- **Video:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=eaQPi0LDoE0

### Complex C: Bodyweight Power

- **Rounds:** 3
- **Work:** 5 explosive pull-ups (chest to bar if possible) → 10 Hindu push-ups → 10 jump squats (full depth) → 30s rest (~6 min per complex)
- **New:** Yes
- **Why:** Hindu push-ups combine shoulder mobility with pressing — wrestling conditioning staple.
- **Cues:** Pull-ups: pull fast, clear the bar. Hindu PU: downward dog → swoop chest forward through → cobra → reverse.
- **Progression:** Pull-ups → chest-to-bar → muscle-up negatives. Hindu PU: slow swoop to 3s.
- **Visual positions:** Downward dog → Swoop through → Cobra
- **Video:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=lTzaiPM82Ps

**Day note:** Quality over quantity. Form breaks = stop the set. At 91 kg, 5 explosive pull-ups for 3 rounds is demanding.

- **Sets × Reps:** 2 × 10
- **Load:** Bodyweight
- **Rest:** 45 sec
- **New:** Yes
- **Why:** Activates lower traps and serratus. Shoulder insurance before heavy pulling.
- **Cues:** Hang with straight arms. Retract + depress shoulder blades — rise a few inches without bending elbows. Hold 1s at top.
- **Progression:** Add 3s hold at top. Then single-arm assisted.
- **Visual positions:** Passive (shoulders by ears) → Engaged (squeeze down — scapulae retract)

- **8-week target:** +15 kg × 5
- **New:** No
- **Why:** At 12 BW reps, adding weight is the next stimulus. This will push BW max back toward 18.
- **Cues:** Full dead hang at bottom. Chin clearly over bar. 2s descent minimum.
- **Progression:** +2.5 kg when all sets hit 5 cleanly.

### 3. Archer Pull-Ups

- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 3 each side
- **Load:** Bodyweight (91 kg)
- **Rest:** 90 sec
- **Fallback:** Band-assisted on straight arm if too hard
- **New:** Yes
- **Why:** Bridge between regular and one-arm pull-ups. Unilateral pulling at 91 kg is demanding.
- **Cues:** Wide grip. Pull toward one hand while the other arm straightens fully. Alternate sides. Control negative.
- **Progression:** 3×3 → 3×4 → 3×5 → typewriter pull-ups.
- **Visual positions:** Pull to one side (assist arm straight)

### 4. Barbell Curls

- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 10
- **Weight:** 30 kg barbell (or 14 kg dumbbells)

- **Rest:** 60 sec
- **New:** No
- **Why:** Bicep strength supports all pulling and protects elbow joints.
- **Cues:** Straight bar or EZ bar. Full range of motion. No swinging — if momentum needed, weight is too heavy.
- **Progression:** Alternate barbell / dumbbell / hammer curls across weeks.

### 5. Face Pulls (Cable)

- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 15
- **Equipment:** Cable machine with rope attachment
- **Cable height:** Face height (~170 cm for 1.93m)
- **Weight:** 12.5 kg (start light — this is about control)
- **Hold:** 2 sec at peak contraction
- **Rest:** 45 sec
- **New:** Yes
- **Why:** Targets rear delts and external rotators. The single most important exercise for shoulder longevity that most people skip.
- **Cues:** Pull rope to ears, externally rotate at end position — thumbs should point backward. Squeeze 2s.
- **Progression:** +2.5 kg when 15 reps with 2s hold feel easy. Eventually single-arm.
- **Note:** Start lighter than you think. The external rotation at the end is the money part, not the weight.
- **Visual positions:** Pull to face (thumbs back, external rotation)
- **Video:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rep-qVOkqgk

### 6. Skin the Cat

- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 3, as slow as possible
- **Equipment:** Rings (preferred) or pull-up bar
- **Load:** Bodyweight (91 kg)
- **Rest:** 90 sec
- **New:** Yes
- **Why:** Builds bulletproof shoulders through full range of motion under load. Outstanding for mobility and strength.
- **Cues:** Tuck knees, rotate body backward through arms. Start with first half only (to German hang position). Only go as far as comfortable. SLOW.
- **Progression:** Tuck → straight legs → pause in German hang 5s → full rotation.
- **Note:** At 91 kg this is heavy for skin the cat. Start with partial ROM. No rushing — this is where injuries happen.
- **Visual positions:** Hang → Tuck through → German hang
- **Video:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=QHnpUcVwZOk

### 7. High-Knee Rope Fartlek

- **Sets × Reps:** 5 × 45s max-effort high knees / 30s easy step (~6 min total)
- **Rope:** Normal speed rope
- **HR target:** >160 bpm during work phases
- **New:** Yes
- **Why:** Fills the mid-week Fartlek gap. High-knee sprint maximises hip flexor recruitment and VO2 stimulus without the joint load of running. Short intense blocks preserve quality after heavy pulling work.
- **Cues:** Drive knees to hip height — not shuffling, genuinely high. Max cadence on the sprint phase. Easy single-bounce step on recovery. Reset posture before each sprint.
- **Progression:** 5 × 45s/30s → 6 × 45s/30s → 5 × 60s/30s → add double-unders on sprint phase.

### 8. Rope Skipping Cooldown

- **Duration:** 5 min, easy pace
- **Rope:** Normal speed rope
- **Pace:** Zone 1 — conversational
- **New:** No
- **Why:** Active recovery after Fartlek. Brings heart rate down gradually.
- **Cues:** Rhythm focus. Not a workout.
- **Progression:** Stays easy.

**Day note:** Scapular pull-ups first — always. Face pulls are the most important exercise here for longevity. High-knee Fartlek fills the Thursday cardio gap — keep the sprint phases genuinely fast. Your 12 clean pull-ups at 91 kg = solid base for weighted work.

## Friday — Lower Body + Conditioning (~50 min)

### 1. KB Goblet Squat

- **Sets × Reps:** 4 × 10, 3s pause at bottom
- **Weight:** 24 kg (target weight)
- **Depth:** As deep as mobility allows — break parallel
- **Rest:** 90 sec
- **New:** No
- **Why:** Builds squat depth and strength. Pause eliminates stretch reflex and forces honest reps.
- **Cues:** KB at chest. Elbows inside knees at bottom. 3s pause in the hole. Drive up through heels.
- **Progression:** 24 kg × 10 pause → 24 kg × 12 pause → double KB front squat.
- **Note:** Bench/pull-up strength says 24 kg is manageable here. If pause makes it too hard, use 20 kg and build.
- **Video:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MeIiIdhvXT4

### 2. Bulgarian Split Squat

- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 8 each leg
- **Load:** 2 × 14 kg dumbbells (start)
- **Rest:** 60 sec between legs, 90 sec between sets
- **Depth:** Back knee to ~5 cm from floor
- **New:** Yes
- **Why:** Single-leg strength builder. Exposes and fixes imbalances. More knee-friendly than heavy barbell squats for 45+.
- **Cues:** Rear foot on bench, shoelaces down. Front shin roughly vertical. Hold DBs at sides.
- **Progression:** BW → 2×14 kg DB → 2×18 kg DB → 2×22 kg DB → add deficit (front foot elevated).
- **Note:** Long femurs (1.93m) = harder split squats. Start lighter than ego says. 14 kg DBs + 91 kg BW is plenty.
- **Visual positions:** Bottom position (rear foot on bench)
- **Video:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2C-uNgKwPLE

### 3. Kettlebell Swing

- **Sets × Reps:** 4 × 15, Russian style (eye level)
- **Weight:** 24 kg (target)
- **Rest:** 60 sec
- **New:** No
- **Why:** Posterior chain power. Hip hinge builds glutes and hamstrings explosively.
- **Cues:** Swing to eye level. Snap hips — arms just along for the ride. Squeeze glutes at top.
- **Progression:** 24 kg × 15 → 24 kg × 20 → single-arm 20 kg.
- **Video:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YSxHifyI6s8

### 4. Nordic Curl Negative

- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 5, target 5s descent per rep (~4 min total)
- **Load:** Bodyweight (91 kg)
- **Descent target:** 5 sec (3 sec is fine to start)
- **Anchor:** Under loaded barbell, or partner holds ankles
- **Rest:** 90 sec
- **New:** Yes
- **Why:** Best hamstring exercise that exists. Dramatically reduces injury risk. Eccentric-only initially.
- **Cues:** Kneel on pad, anchor feet. Lower yourself as slowly as possible. Catch with hands at bottom. Push back up to start.
- **Progression:** 3s negative → 5s → 8s → half-range concentric → full Nordic curl.
- **Note:** Most people at 91 kg can't do a 5s negative on day 1. Aim for 3s, build to 5s over 3 weeks. Being humbled is normal.
- **Visual positions:** Start (kneel upright) → 5s negative (slow fall) → Catch (hands on ground)
- **Video:** https://youtube.com/watch?v=_e9vFU9-tkc

### 5. Calf Raises

- **Sets × Reps:** 3 × 15, slow tempo
- **Load:** Bodyweight to start, then +20 kg DB
- **Tempo:** 2s up, 2s hold at top, 2s down, 2s stretch at bottom
- **Rest:** 45 sec
- **New:** No
- **Why:** Calf and Achilles tendon health. Slow tempo loads tendon properly.
- **Cues:** Full stretch at bottom. Full contraction at top. Off a step for extra range.
- **Progression:** Double leg → single leg → add weight → off a step.

### 6. 45 Min Elliptical (Fartlek)

- **Sets × Reps:** 45 min continuous
- **Structure:** Level 22 base OR ladder up/down intervals
- **Resistance:** Push intensity via levels instead of speed to spike heart rate
- **Total time:** 45 min
- **New:** No
- **Why:** Low-impact Fartlek-style training. Builds a massive aerobic engine and muscular endurance while completely sparing the joints.
- **Cues:** Use high resistance (e.g., Level 22) as your base. Climb or drop resistance strategically.
- **Progression:** Increase your baseline resistance level or extend the duration of the high-intensity rungs of your ladder.

**Day note:** Nordic curls will humble you at 91 kg — that's 91 kg your hamstrings are decelerating. Respect the movement. Bulgarians expose imbalances you didn't know you had.

- **Progression:** Empty hand → shoe on fist → 12 kg → 16 kg → 20 kg → 24 kg.
- **Note:** KB strength says you could press 24 kg. But TGU pattern needs learning. Start 12 kg for 2 weeks, then 16, then 20.

### 3. Handstand Wall Hold

- **Rest:** 60 sec
- **Fallback:** Pike position with feet on box
- **New:** Yes
- **Why:** Overhead stability, wrist strength, body awareness. Real fall-prevention benefit at 50+.
- **Cues:** Chest facing wall, hands ~15 cm from wall. Squeeze glutes and core. Breathe. If too hard: pike position with feet elevated on box.
- **Progression:** Wall hold 30s → toe pulls (one foot off wall) → freestanding attempts → handstand push-up negatives.
- **Note:** Tall (1.93m) + heavy (91 kg) makes handstands significantly harder. Pike on box is a valid regression. No shame.

### 4. Thoracic Spine Rotation

- **Duration:** 2 min each side
- **New:** Yes
- **Why:** Counters stiffness from desk work and age in the mid-back. Opens up breathing and overhead positions.
- **Cues:** Lie on side, knees stacked and bent 90°. Open top arm across body and rotate, following with eyes. Let gravity do the work.
- **Progression:** Add a light reach at end range. Then try quadruped T-spine rotations.

- **Duration:** Accumulate 3 min total (break up as needed)
- **Heel elevation:** 5 kg plates under heels if heels lift
- **Focus:** Relaxation, not effort
- **New:** Yes
- **Why:** Maintaining this resting position is one of the best things for hip, ankle, and lower back mobility. Most adults lose it by 40.
- **Cues:** Heels down (plates if needed). Hold something for balance initially. Shift weight side to side. Breathe and relax into it.

- **Note:** Height (1.93m) means ankle dorsiflexion is the likely limiter (longer tibias). Heel plates are not cheating — they're the smart progression.

## Key Programming Principles

### Age-Specific (51)

- **Tendon loading:** Tendons adapt 3–6× slower than muscles. Tempo work (3s eccentrics, pause squats, Nordic negatives) specifically strengthens connective tissue.
- **Power training:** Plyometric push-ups and explosive pull-ups are essential — power output declines ~2× faster than strength after 40. Even 2–3 sets of low-rep explosive work per week slows this meaningfully.
- **Anti-rotation and decompression:** Pallof press, dead hangs, and Turkish get-ups protect spinal health — if your back goes, everything stops.

### Height-Specific (1.93m)

- **L-sits:** Longer lever arms = mechanically harder. Tuck is fine.
- **Handstands:** More bodyweight to support inverted. Pike on box is valid.
- **Bulgarian split squats:** Long femurs increase difficulty. Start lighter.
- **Deep squat sit:** Longer tibias need more ankle dorsiflexion. Use heel plates.
- **Rowing:** Longer stroke = natural advantage. Exploit it.

### Weight-Specific (91 kg)

- **Nordic curls:** 91 kg is serious load for eccentrics. 3s negative is fine to start.
- **Skin the cat:** Heavy for the movement. Partial ROM first.
- **Calisthenics generally:** BW moves are harder at 91 kg. Adjust expectations vs. lighter athletes.

### Free Weights vs. Cables

- Free weights preferred for: bench, floor press, pull-ups, curls, KB work, all bodyweight movements.
- Cables preferred for: Pallof press (constant tension anti-rotation), face pulls (tension curve matches the movement). These two exercises are genuinely better on cables.

### Deload Week (Week 4)

- Cut volume by 40% (fewer sets, not fewer exercises)
- Keep intensity the same (same weights, same movements)
- Example: 4×6 bench becomes 2×6. 3×8 ring dips becomes 2×8.
- All exercises still performed, just less total volume.
- Non-negotiable at 51. Your VO2max says engine is great; chassis needs maintenance.
