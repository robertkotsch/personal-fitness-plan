# Rob's Training Plan — Restructured

## What Changed and Why

### Structural fixes

1. **Wednesday ↔ Tuesday swap.** HIIT (with explosive pull-ups) no longer sits the day before heavy pull day. The new order gives 48h between pulling sessions instead of 24h.
2. **Horizontal row added to Thursday.** Cable or ring row fills the missing movement pattern. Balances push:pull ratio and protects shoulders.
3. **Thursday trimmed from 8 exercises to 7.** Rope cooldown merged into the Fartlek block (extend easy phase). Skin the cat stays but moves earlier when you're fresher — it's the highest-risk movement.
4. **Saturday reframed honestly.** Burpee Fartlek is conditioning, not "movement quality." Mobility work separated into its own block with a clear boundary.
5. **Single-leg RDL added to Friday.** Addresses the hamstring gap beyond Nordics. Light, eccentric-friendly, works balance simultaneously.
6. **Second lower-body touchpoint on Wednesday.** Goblet squat warm-up before KB complex gives legs twice-weekly frequency without adding a session.

### What stayed the same

All original exercises, loads, tempos, progressions, and periodization are preserved unless specifically noted. The changes are architectural, not philosophical.

## Tuesday — HIIT Complexes (~40 min)

*Moved from Wednesday. Rationale: explosive pull-ups in Complex C now have 48h recovery before Thursday's heavy pull day instead of 24h.*

Format: 3 complexes, 3 rounds each, 2 min rest between complexes.

### Complex A: Rope & Burpee
- 4 rounds: 40s power rope skip (2 lb heavy) → 10 burpees → 20s rest

### Complex B: Kettlebell Circuit
- 3 rounds: 5 clean & press each arm → 12 swings → 5 goblet squats (2s pause) → 30s rest
- KB: 20 kg → 24 kg
- **Change:** The goblet squats here now serve double duty as a second weekly lower-body touchpoint. Emphasize depth and pause.

### Complex C: Bodyweight Power
- 3 rounds: 5 explosive pull-ups → 10 Hindu push-ups → 10 jump squats → 30s rest

## Thursday — Upper Pull + Skill (~60 min)

*Reduced from ~65 min. Three changes: (1) skin the cat moved to position 3 when grip is fresh and fatigue is low — this is the highest-injury-risk movement at 91 kg, (2) cable row added as the missing horizontal pull, (3) rope Fartlek and cooldown merged into one 10-min block.*

1. **Scapular Pull-Ups** — 2×10 BW (warm-up, always first)

2. **Weighted Pull-Ups** — 4×5 @ RPE 7–8 (W1: +7.5 kg → W3: +12.5 kg)

3. **Skin the Cat** — 3×3, slow as possible, rings
   - *Moved up from position 6.* Grip and shoulder fatigue from archers + curls was accumulating before the most technically demanding movement. Now performed when fresh. Partial ROM still applies at 91 kg.

4. **Archer Pull-Ups** — 3×3 each side BW

5. **Cable Row** ← NEW
   - **Sets × Reps:** 3×10
   - **Equipment:** Seated cable row, close-grip or V-bar attachment
   - **Weight:** Start ~45 kg (adjust to RPE 7)
   - **Tempo:** 2s pull, 1s squeeze at chest, 2s return
   - **Rest:** 60 sec
   - **Why:** The original plan has zero horizontal pulling. You're benching, floor pressing, and doing ring dips (all horizontal push) with no rowing counterpart. This imbalance stresses the anterior shoulder over time. Cable row loads the mid-back, rhomboids, and rear delts in a way face pulls alone can't.
   - **Alternative:** Ring rows (3×10, feet elevated) if you prefer bodyweight. Pendlay rows if you want barbell.
   - **Cues:** Chest stays tall. Pull to lower sternum. Squeeze shoulder blades 1s. No momentum.
   - **Progression:** +5 kg when 3×10 with 1s squeeze feels controlled.

6. **Barbell Curls** — 3×10, 30 kg, 2s up / 2s down

7. **Face Pulls** — 3×15, cable, 2s hold at peak contraction

8. **High-Knee Rope Fartlek + Cooldown** — 6×60s max-effort / 30s easy step, then 3 min easy skipping to finish (~12 min total)
   - *Merged the separate 5-min cooldown into this block.* Instead of a distinct cooldown exercise, extend the final 2 rest intervals to 60s easy pace. Same training effect, one fewer "exercise" mentally.

**Note on skin the cat removal consideration:** I kept it because it's genuinely excellent for shoulder health long-term. But if Thursday still feels too dense after 2 cycles, skin the cat is the first thing to move to Saturday's skill block. Monitor grip fatigue as the signal.

## Friday — Lower Body + Conditioning (~55 min)

*One addition: single-leg RDL after Bulgarians. Addresses the hamstring gap — Nordics are eccentric-only at your stage and very low volume (3×5). This adds a hip-hinge hamstring stimulus that also trains balance.*

1. **KB Goblet Squat** — 4×10, 24 kg, 3s pause at bottom

2. **Bulgarian Split Squat** — 3×8 each leg, 2×14 kg DB

3. **Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift** ← NEW
   - **Sets × Reps:** 3×8 each leg
   - **Weight:** 20 kg KB or DB (start light — balance is the limiter)
   - **Tempo:** 2s down, 1s at bottom, controlled up
   - **Rest:** 60 sec between legs
   - **Why:** Your only hamstring work is Nordic negatives (3×5 eccentric-only) and KB swings (ballistic, not hypertrophy). SLRDL fills the gap: lengthened-position hamstring work + single-leg balance + hip stability. At 1.93m, proprioception challenge is significant.
   - **Cues:** Hinge at hip, free leg goes straight back. KB in opposite hand to working leg. Slight knee bend on standing leg. Touch KB to mid-shin level. Don't rotate hips — square to floor.
   - **Progression:** 20 kg → 24 kg → 28 kg. Then deficit (stand on 5 cm plate).
   - **Note:** This is also fall-prevention work disguised as a hamstring exercise. Single-leg stability under load is one of the most functional things you can train at 51.

4. **KB Swing** — 4×15, 24 kg, Russian style

5. **Nordic Curl Negative** — 3×5, target 5s descent

6. **Calf Raises** — 3×15, slow tempo (2-2-2-2)

7. **45 Min Elliptical Fartlek** — Level 22 base, ladder intervals

## Saturday — Long Conditioning + Mobility (~60 min)

*Reframed. The original called this "Long Aerobic + Movement Quality" but 160 burpees across an hour is conditioning. Calling it what it is. Mobility work is separated into a distinct post-conditioning block so it actually gets done properly rather than being squeezed between burpees and TGUs.*

### Block A: Conditioning (40–45 min)
1. **1-Hour Rope & Burpee Fartlek** — 60 min continuous, 8×20 burpees mixed in, heavy/normal rope alternating

### Block B: Skill & Mobility (15–20 min)
2. **Turkish Get-Up** — 3×2 each side, 12 kg start (skill work, rest as needed)
3. **Handstand Wall Hold** — 3×20–30s (pike fallback)
4. **Thoracic Spine Rotation** — 2 min each side
5. **Deep Squat Sit** — Accumulate 3 min total

**Block B note:** If Block A wipes you out (and some weeks it will, especially W3), do Block B first or move it to Sunday morning. Mobility done exhausted is mobility done badly. The TGU especially requires focus, not survival.

## What I Didn't Change

- Periodization (Moderate → Hard → Very Hard → Deload)
- All loads, tempos, RPE targets, and progression schemes
- Exercise selection philosophy (free weights > cables, except Pallof + face pulls)
- Deload protocol (−40% volume, keep intensity)
- Every original exercise is still in the plan
