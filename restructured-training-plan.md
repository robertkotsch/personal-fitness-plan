## Monday — Upper Push + Power (~78 min)

1. **Easy Rope Skipping (warm-up)** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kDOGb9C5kp0) — 5 min, Zone 1, conversational pace
   - **Why:** Cold-starting plyo push-ups at 91 kg and 51 years old is asking for trouble. 5 min easy rope gets blood into the shoulders, wrists, and pecs before loading.

2. **KB Halo** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://youtube.com/watch?v=13SFATc-mJ4) — 2×12 each direction, 20 kg
   - **Why:** Warms up shoulders and thoracic spine.

3. **Bench Press (Tempo)** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vcBig73ojpE) — 4×6 @ RPE 7–8, 3-1-X-0 tempo (W1: 72.5 kg → W3: 77.5 kg)
   - **Why:** Primary horizontal push. Tempo-controlled for tendon health.

4. **Plyometric Push-Ups** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://youtube.com/watch?v=FFlAcBTd_K0) — 3×5 BW
   - **Why:** Power output declines ~2× faster than strength after 40. Low-rep explosive work counters this.

5. **Ring Dips** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://youtube.com/watch?v=AdUfoCj3COQ) — 3×8 BW
   - **Why:** Rings let wrists rotate naturally — more shoulder-friendly. Adds stabilizer demand.

6. **Floor Press** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=uE3lWIDTExo) — 3×10, 2×28 kg DB
   - **Why:** Limits ROM to protect shoulders while overloading triceps. Great for 50+ lifters.

7. **Heavy Rope Finisher** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gC82dD9_p20) — 6×60s on / 15s off (~7.5 min)
   - **Why:** 40s/20s is too easy for 6k+ rope endurance. Lengthened sprint and shortened rest creates real lactic challenge.

8. **Zone 2 Cooldown** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kDOGb9C5kp0) — 18 min easy rope skipping or assault bike, HR 130–140 bpm
   - **Why:** Post-lactic Zone 2 work is one of the most efficient ways to build aerobic base. Your HR is already elevated from the finisher — riding that elevated baseline at Zone 2 for 18 min adds meaningful cardiac output without CNS cost.
   - **Cues:** Easy enough to hold a conversation. If using rope, single bounce at relaxed rhythm. If assault bike, low resistance steady cadence.
   - **Progression:** Extend to 20 min, then 25 min if time allows.

## Tuesday — HIIT Complexes + Core (~81 min)

### Warm-Up (~8 min)

1. **KB Halo** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://youtube.com/watch?v=13SFATc-mJ4) — 2×8 each direction, 20 kg (~2 min)
   - **Why:** Active shoulder and thoracic warm-up before explosive work.

2. **Easy Rope Skipping** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kDOGb9C5kp0) — 5 min, Zone 1 (~5 min)
   - **Why:** You're about to do heavy rope power skipping, explosive pull-ups, and jump squats. Warming up shoulders, wrists, and ankles first is non-negotiable.

### Complexes (~32 min)

Format: 3 complexes, 3–4 rounds each, 2 min rest between complexes.

#### Complex A: Rope & Burpee

- 4 rounds: 40s power rope skip (2 lb heavy) [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=gC82dD9_p20) → 10 burpees [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TUdZq_2bXyU) → 20s rest
- **Why:** Shifts stimulus from pure cardio endurance to massive upper-body/lat pre-exhaustion before hitting the burpees.

#### Complex B: Kettlebell Circuit

- 3 rounds: 5 clean & press each arm [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://youtube.com/watch?v=eaQPi0LDoE0) → 12 swings [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K83RM7H9WrY) → 5 goblet squats [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lRYBbchqxtI) (2s pause) → 30s rest
- KB: 20 kg → 24 kg
- **Why:** Clean & press is full-body power, easier on wrists than barbell cleans.
- **Note:** The goblet squats here serve double duty as a second weekly lower-body touchpoint. Emphasize depth and pause.

#### Complex C: Bodyweight Power

- 3 rounds: 5 explosive pull-ups [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=V7cT5m8Hq_M) → 10 Hindu push-ups [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://youtube.com/watch?v=lTzaiPM82Ps) → 10 jump squats [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vB7w6S7V-uA) → 30s rest
- **Why:** Hindu push-ups combine shoulder mobility with pressing — wrestling conditioning staple. Explosive pull-ups maintain power output.

### Core Circuit (~8 min)

3. **Hanging Knee Raises** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=VPxUQiH-kU) — 3×10, controlled (no kipping)
   - **Why:** Dynamic core flexion complements Wednesday's anti-movement core work (Pallof press, L-sit). Hanging position also decompresses spine after the complexes.
   - **Cues:** Knees to chest, 1s hold at top, 2s lower. No swinging.
   - **Progression:** Knee raises → straight leg raises → toes to bar.

4. **Hollow Body Hold** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6J6_2sNq2iA) — 3×20s
   - **Why:** Full-body anterior chain tension. Transfers to handstand holds, L-sits, and ring work.
   - **Cues:** Lower back pressed into floor. Arms overhead, legs extended, toes pointed. If too hard, bend knees or bring arms to sides.
   - **Progression:** 20s → 30s → 40s → add light ankle weights.

### Steady-State Conditioning (~20 min)

5. **Rowing** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zQ82RYIFLN8) — 20 min, Zone 2, pace ~2:05–2:10/500m
   - **Why:** Post-HIIT aerobic work is excellent for recovery and aerobic development. Your HR is already elevated — 20 min at Zone 2 (130–140 bpm) capitalizes on that without adding training stress. Also serves as the active recovery transition.
   - **Cues:** Long strokes, controlled breathing. This is NOT another interval — keep it conversational.
   - **Progression:** Track total distance. Aim to hold a consistent pace throughout.

### Cooldown (~5 min)

6. **Easy Rope Skipping** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kDOGb9C5kp0) — 5 min, Zone 1
   - **Why:** Gradual HR reduction. Active recovery for wrists and shoulders after the heavy rope complex.

## Wednesday — Rowing Engine + Core & Mobility (~81 min)

1. **40 Min Rowing Fartlek** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=zQ82RYIFLN8) — 1 min max sprint every 5 min, ~9,000m target
   - **Why:** Fartlek-style interval training. Builds VO2max during the sprints while maintaining a strong aerobic base during recovery. Extended from 30 min — Wednesday is the aerobic development day and 30 min was leaving capacity on the table.
   - **Note:** Sprint intervals stay at 1 min every 5 min. That's now 8 sprints instead of 6.

2. **KB Halo** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://youtube.com/watch?v=13SFATc-mJ4) — 2×10 each direction, 20 kg
   - **Why:** Active recovery. Transition to core work.

3. **Farmer's Walk** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FvdO-nzjAFA) — 3×40m, 2×24 kg KB, 60s rest (~5 min)
   - **Why:** Low-CNS loaded carry that trains grip, core stability, and posture under load. Perfect for a buffer day — no eccentric damage, no joint stress, just honest full-body work. Also reinforces the upright trunk position you need for squats and deadlifts.
   - **Cues:** Shoulders packed down and back. Walk in a straight line. Don't let KBs touch thighs. Breathe.
   - **Progression:** 2×24 kg → 2×28 kg → 2×32 kg. Then extend distance to 60m.

4. **Dead Hang** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M25ibDAVhMQ) — 3×45s (decompression, not training stimulus)
   - **Why:** Decompresses spine after rowing. Builds grip. Shoulder health.

5. **L-Sit Progression** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=IUZJoSP66HI) — 3×15–20s hold (tuck is fine)
   - **Why:** Deep core engagement that sit-ups never reach. Builds hip flexor strength.

6. **Pallof Press** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AH_QZLm_0-s) — 3×10 each side, cable, 2s hold at extension
   - **Why:** Anti-rotation core exercise. Trains core's actual job: resisting unwanted movement. Crucial for spinal health.

7. **Copenhagen Plank** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=S3742-WICz8) — 2×20s each side, 45s rest (~3 min)
   - **Why:** There is zero adductor work in this plan, and you're doing Bulgarian split squats and single-leg RDLs on Friday. The adductors stabilize the knee during all single-leg movements — weak adductors under heavy single-leg load is an injury vector. This is isometric prehab, not training.
   - **Cues:** Side plank position, top leg on bench, bottom leg hangs. Lift bottom leg to meet bench. Hold. If too hard: bent knee on bench instead of straight leg.
   - **Progression:** 20s → 30s → 40s. Then straight-leg version if starting with bent knee.

8. **Shoulder Dislocates** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=A_0uY-eF52g) — 2×15, band or dowel (~3 min)
   - **Why:** Prepares shoulders for Thursday's heavy pulling. Increases overhead mobility gradually. At 91 kg and desk-job posture, anterior shoulder tightness will limit skin the cat and overhead positions.
   - **Cues:** Wide grip on band/dowel. Slow controlled arc from front to behind back. Narrow grip progressively as mobility improves.
   - **Progression:** Narrow grip width over weeks.

9. **90/90 Hip Stretch** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kU11Nl8X4s8) — 2 min each side
   - **Why:** Hip mobility degrades silently after 40. Best position for internal + external rotation.

10. **Extended Mobility** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=426rzPkx4D8) — 10 min foam rolling or lacrosse ball work
    - **Focus areas:** Lats (supports overhead positions), T-spine (supports pulling and pressing), quads/hip flexors (supports squat depth)
    - **Why:** You have 10 min of budget and a 51-year-old body. Use it. This is the one session per week where you have time and low fatigue for proper tissue work.

## Thursday — Upper Pull + Skill (~74 min)

### Warm-Up (~7 min)

1. **Easy Rope Skipping** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kDOGb9C5kp0) — 5 min, Zone 1
   - **Why:** Gets blood flowing to shoulders, wrists, and grip before heavy pulling.

2. **Band Pull-Aparts** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9M8ylnbriB0) — 2×15, light band
   - **Why:** Activates rear delts and external rotators before heavy pulling. Scapular pull-ups are already in the plan but start with straight arms — band pull-aparts wake up the retraction pattern first.

### Main Work (~43 min)

3. **Scapular Pull-Ups** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=9M8ylnbriB0) — 2×10 BW (warm-up, always first)
   - **Why:** Activates lower traps and serratus. Shoulder insurance before heavy pulling.

4. **Weighted Pull-Ups** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=eGo4IYlbE5g) — 4×5 @ RPE 7–8 (W1: +7.5 kg → W3: +12.5 kg)
   - **Why:** At 12 BW reps, adding weight is the next stimulus. This will push BW max back toward 18.

5. **Skin the Cat** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QHnpUcVwZOk) — 3×3, slow as possible, rings
   - **Why:** Builds bulletproof shoulders through full range of motion under load. Outstanding for mobility and strength. Performed at position 3 (instead of after curls) when grip is fresh and fatigue is low — this is the highest-injury-risk movement at 91 kg.

6. **Archer Pull-Ups** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6qtY8j92gA8) — 3×3 each side BW
   - **Why:** Bridge between regular and one-arm pull-ups. Unilateral pulling at 91 kg is demanding.

7. **Cable Row** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=jG394Q6J9J4)
   - **Sets × Reps:** 3×10
   - **Equipment:** Seated cable row, close-grip or V-bar attachment
   - **Weight:** Start ~45 kg (adjust to RPE 7)
   - **Tempo:** 2s pull, 1s squeeze at chest, 2s return
   - **Rest:** 60 sec
   - **Why:** The original plan has zero horizontal pulling. You're benching, floor pressing, and doing ring dips (all horizontal push) with no rowing counterpart. This imbalance stresses the anterior shoulder over time. Cable row loads the mid-back, rhomboids, and rear delts in a way face pulls alone can't.
   - **Alternative:** Ring rows (3×10, feet elevated) if you prefer bodyweight. Pendlay rows if you want barbell.
   - **Cues:** Chest stays tall. Pull to lower sternum. Squeeze shoulder blades 1s. No momentum.
   - **Progression:** +5 kg when 3×10 with 1s squeeze feels controlled.

8. **Barbell Curls** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=i1YgFZB6alI) — 3×10, 30 kg, 2s up / 2s down
   - **Why:** Bicep strength supports all pulling and protects elbow joints.

9. **Face Pulls** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qfc70k40318) — 3×15, cable, 2s hold at peak contraction
   - **Why:** Targets rear delts and external rotators. The single most important exercise for shoulder longevity that most people skip.

### Decompression (~2 min)

10. **Dead Hang** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M25ibDAVhMQ) — 2×30s, passive
    - **Why:** Spine decompression after heavy weighted pull-ups (98–103 kg total load) and cable rows. Not a training stimulus — just letting gravity undo the compression from all that loaded pulling.

### Conditioning (~17 min)

11. **High-Knee Rope Fartlek + Cooldown** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xcIfssjP-u8) — 8×60s max-effort / 30s easy step, then 5 min easy skipping to finish (~17 min total)
    - **Why:** High-knee sprint maximizes hip flexor recruitment and VO2 stimulus without the joint load of running. Short intense blocks preserve quality after heavy pulling work.

### Shoulder Stretching (~5 min)

12. **Cross-Body Shoulder Stretch** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qhR568M6XAQ) — 30s each side
13. **Doorframe Pec Stretch** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qhR568M6XAQ) — 30s each side
14. **Band-Assisted Overhead Stretch** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=qhR568M6XAQ) — 30s each side
    - **Why:** After 4 heavy pulling movements + rows + curls, shoulders and lats are shortened and tight. 5 min of targeted stretching returns tissue to resting length and prepares for tomorrow's lower body session.

## Friday — Lower Body + Conditioning (~90 min)

1. **KB Goblet Squat** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lRYBbchqxtI) — 4×10, 24 kg, 3s pause at bottom
   - **Why:** Builds squat depth and strength. Pause eliminates stretch reflex and forces honest reps.

2. **Bulgarian Split Squat** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kU8O3yqE-O8) — 3×8 each leg, 2×14 kg DB
   - **Why:** Single-leg strength builder. Exposes and fixes imbalances. More knee-friendly than heavy barbell squats for 45+.

3. **Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=aJq97nKzVqA)
   - **Sets × Reps:** 3×8 each leg
   - **Weight:** 20 kg KB or DB (start light — balance is the limiter)
   - **Tempo:** 2s down, 1s at bottom, controlled up
   - **Rest:** 60 sec between legs
   - **Why:** Your only hamstring work is Nordic negatives (3×5 eccentric-only) and KB swings (ballistic, not hypertrophy). SLRDL fills the gap: lengthened-position hamstring work + single-leg balance + hip stability. At 1.93m, proprioception challenge is significant.
   - **Cues:** Hinge at hip, free leg goes straight back. KB in opposite hand to working leg. Slight knee bend on standing leg. Touch KB to mid-shin level. Don't rotate hips — square to floor.
   - **Progression:** 20 kg → 24 kg → 28 kg. Then deficit (stand on 5 cm plate).
   - **Note:** This is also fall-prevention work disguised as a hamstring exercise. Single-leg stability under load is one of the most functional things you can train at 51.

4. **KB Swing** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K83RM7H9WrY) — 4×15, 24 kg, Russian style
   - **Why:** Posterior chain power. Hip hinge builds glutes and hamstrings explosively.

5. **Nordic Curl Negative** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=_e9vFU9-tkc) — 3×5, target 5s descent
   - **Why:** Best hamstring exercise that exists. Dramatically reduces injury risk. Eccentric-only initially.

6. **Calf Raises** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-qsRtp_PbVM) — 3×15, slow tempo (2-2-2-2)
   - **Why:** Calf and Achilles tendon health. Slow tempo loads tendon properly.

7. **45 Min Elliptical Fartlek** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sHMemwz_HPU) — Level 22 base, ladder intervals
   - **Why:** Low-impact Fartlek-style training. Builds a massive aerobic engine and muscular endurance while completely sparing the joints.

## Saturday — Long Conditioning + Mobility (~80 min)

### Block A: Conditioning (~60 min)

1. **1-Hour Rope & Burpee Fartlek** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kDOGb9C5kp0) — 60 min continuous, 8×20 burpees mixed in, heavy/normal rope alternating
   - **Why:** Advanced Fartlek interval training. Builds stamina and VO2max by spiking heart rate with burpees during a long aerobic baseline.

### Block B: Skill & Mobility (15–20 min)

2. **Turkish Get-Up** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=0bWRPC49-KI) — 3×2 each side, 12 kg start (skill work, rest as needed)
   - **Why:** Floor to standing while holding weight overhead. Tests and builds everything: shoulder stability, hip mobility, core, balance. The "am I aging well?" exercise.

3. **Handstand Wall Hold** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=r0rLCKvcqnQ) — 3×20–30s (pike fallback)
   - **Why:** Overhead stability, wrist strength, body awareness. Real fall-prevention benefit at 50+.

4. **Thoracic Spine Rotation** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=426rzPkx4D8) — 2 min each side
   - **Why:** Counters stiffness from desk work and age in the mid-back. Opens up breathing and overhead positions.

5. **Deep Squat Sit** [🎥 Video Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=BTfEFDXp-cw) — Accumulate 3 min total
   - **Why:** Maintaining this resting position is one of the best things for hip, ankle, and lower back mobility. Most adults lose it by 40.

**Block B note:** If Block A wipes you out (and some weeks it will, especially W3), do Block B first or move it to Sunday morning. Mobility done exhausted is mobility done badly. The TGU especially requires focus, not survival.

---

## Sunday — Rest & Recovery

Walk, stretch, do nothing structured. Recovery is where adaptation happens.
