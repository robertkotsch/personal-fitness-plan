## Decision Triggers

These are the signals that should prompt a plan adjustment. When you see one, bring it to the next review.

### Progress signals (good — consider advancing)
- All sets of a lift hit target reps at RPE ≤ 6 for 2 consecutive weeks → increase weight
- Skin the cat reaches full tuck ROM with 3s pause → progress to straight legs
- Nordic negative consistently hits 5s → attempt 8s or half-range concentric
- BW pull-ups reach 15+ → retest weighted 1RM, adjust starting loads
- L-sit tuck holds 30s easily → attempt one-leg-extended
- TGU at 12 kg feels automatic → move to 16 kg
- Copenhagen plank holds 40s → add straight-leg version

### Warning signals (bad — consider reducing or adjusting)
- Same weight feels harder (higher RPE) than previous week at same volume → check sleep, nutrition, accumulated fatigue
- Grip fails before target muscle on Thursday → reduce Thursday volume or add rest
- Any sharp pain during skin the cat → stop immediately, regress to dead hangs for 1 week
- Shoulder discomfort on bench or dips that persists between sets → check face pull and cable row volume, consider reducing push volume
- Nordic curl causes knee pain (not hamstring soreness) → pad position or reduce ROM
- Missed 2+ sessions in a cycle → extend the cycle by a week before progressing loads
- Deload week doesn't feel like recovery → you're overreaching; reduce W3 volume next cycle
- Weight drops >1.5 kg/week → eating too little for this training volume

### Structural review triggers (time to revisit the plan)
- Completed 4 full cycles (16 weeks) → full program review; likely need new rep ranges or exercise variation
- A lift has stalled for 2 consecutive cycles → change stimulus (tempo, rep range, or exercise variation)
- Consistent time crunches on specific days → restructure session length
- New injury or persistent pain → modify around it immediately, don't train through
- VO2max retest shows change → adjust conditioning targets
- Body weight change of ±3 kg → recalibrate BW exercise expectations and potentially all loading
